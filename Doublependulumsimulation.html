Problem Statement

⚪━━⚪━━⚪

Challenge

How can we simulate and visualize the chaotic motion of a double pendulum system, which exhibits extreme sensitivity to initial conditions despite being governed by deterministic equations?

Goal: Create a computational model to demonstrate deterministic chaos and visualize the complex trajectories of a double pendulum system

Why is this Important?

  • Demonstrates fundamental concepts in chaos theory
  • Illustrates the "butterfly effect" in mechanical systems
  • Shows limitations of long-term prediction in deterministic systems

Physics Principles Involved

1. Lagrangian Mechanics

Uses energy-based approach to derive equations of motion

L = T - V (Lagrangian = Kinetic Energy - Potential Energy)

2. Coupled Differential Equations

The motion of both pendulums are interconnected through complex non-linear equations

3. Conservation Laws

  • Energy conservation
  • Momentum principles
  • Angular momentum

4. Chaos Theory

  • Sensitivity to initial conditions
  • Deterministic chaos
  • Non-periodic motion

Libraries & Components

📊 NumPy

Purpose: Numerical computations, array operations, and mathematical functions for handling large datasets efficiently

🔣 SymPy

Purpose: Symbolic mathematics for deriving equations of motion analytically using Lagrangian mechanics

🧮 SciPy (odeint)

Purpose: Numerical integration of ordinary differential equations using LSODA algorithm

📈 Matplotlib

Purpose: Creating visualizations, plots, and animations of pendulum motion

Physical Components (Parameters)

  • m₁, m₂: Masses of pendulum bobs
  • L₁, L₂: Lengths of pendulum rods
  • θ₁, θ₂: Angular positions
  • g: Gravitational acceleration (9.81 m/s²)

Algorithm / Plan of Execution

  1. Define System Parameters
    Set masses (m₁, m₂), lengths (L₁, L₂), initial angles (θ₁₀, θ₂₀), and velocities
  2. Symbolic Derivation
    Use SymPy to derive coupled differential equations from Lagrangian mechanics
  3. Convert to Numerical Form
    Transform symbolic equations into numerical functions (lambdify)
  4. Solve Differential Equations
    Use scipy.odeint to integrate the system over time
  5. Convert to Cartesian Coordinates
    Transform angular positions to (x, y) coordinates for visualization
  6. Generate Visualizations
    Create angle-time plots and animated GIF showing pendulum motion

Expected Outcomes

🎯 Successfully simulate chaotic motion of double pendulum system

Visual Outputs

  • Animated GIF showing pendulum trajectory
  • Angle vs Time plots for both pendulums
  • Clear visualization of chaotic behavior

Analytical Results

  • Demonstration of sensitivity to initial conditions
  • Non-periodic, chaotic motion patterns
  • Energy conservation validation

Key Observations

  • Butterfly Effect: Tiny changes in starting angles lead to vastly different trajectories
  • Deterministic Chaos: Motion is predictable by equations but appears random
  • Complex Coupling: Energy transfers between pendulums in irregular patterns
  • Numerical Accuracy: Validate solution accuracy through energy conservation
