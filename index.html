Learn About Projectile Motion

Physics Principles

Projectile motion describes the path of an object thrown or projected into the air. In a vacuum, the trajectory follows a perfect parabola governed by kinematic equations.

Key Equations

Position:

x(t) = v₀ cos(θ) t

y(t) = v₀ sin(θ) t - ½ g t²

With Air Resistance:

Fdrag = -B v |v|

Requires numerical integration to solve

Real-World Applications

  • Sports: Basketball, golf, soccer trajectories
  • Engineering: Ballistics, rocket science
  • Meteorology: Precipitation patterns

Learn About Double Pendulum & Chaos

Physics Principles

The double pendulum is a classic example of a chaotic system. Despite having simple, deterministic equations of motion, its behavior is highly sensitive to initial conditions.

Lagrangian Mechanics

The motion is described by coupled differential equations derived from the Lagrangian (L = T - V):

Complex coupled ODEs involving both angles and angular velocities

Must be solved numerically using methods like Runge-Kutta

Chaos & Sensitivity

The Lyapunov exponent measures how quickly nearby trajectories diverge. A positive exponent indicates chaos.

This means long-term prediction is impossible - tiny measurement errors grow exponentially!

Real-World Examples

  • Weather systems (butterfly effect)
  • Celestial mechanics (three-body problem)
  • Economic systems
  • Population dynamics
